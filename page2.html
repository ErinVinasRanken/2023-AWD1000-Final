Rock, Paper, Scissors FAQ

It is an intrisitive hand game, usually played between two people, in which each player simultaneously forms one of the three shapes with an outstretched hand.

Each hand gesture has a weakness and a strength. Rock beats scissors, but looses to paper. Paper beats rock, but looses to scissors. Scissors beats paper, but looses to rock. you just have to choose the winning gesture to win.

The first known mention of the game was back in the 1600 from a book called Wuzazu by the Ming-dynasty writer Xie Zhaozhe. The name in the book was called Shoushiling, Houzhitou, or Huaquan.
